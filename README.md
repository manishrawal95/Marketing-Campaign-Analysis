# Marketing Campaign Analysis

Customer segmentation and campaign performance analysis on 2,240 customers using RFM modeling and purchase behavior data.

## Questions I was asking

- Which customer segments drive disproportionate revenue — and are the high-spend acquisition campaigns actually targeting them?
- Does campaign response rate vary meaningfully by segment, or is it roughly uniform?
- What behavioral signals separate high-LTV customers from average ones?

## Key findings

- Top 20% of customers contribute 52% of total revenue ($1.36M dataset)
- Overall campaign response rate: 27.2% — but this masks large variance across segments
- Households with children show significantly lower campaign responsiveness despite similar income levels
- Wine and meat products dominate spend for high-LTV segments; fruit and fish are marginal
